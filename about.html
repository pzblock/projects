"A grasshopped filled with dew was merrily singing under a shade;

a whangam, that eats grasshoppers, had marked it for its prey, and was just stretching forth to devour it;

a serpent, that had for a long time fed only on whangams, was coiled up to fasten on the whangam;

a yellow bird was just upon the wing to dart upon the serpent;

a hawk had just stooped from above to seize the yellow bird;

all were intent on their prey, and unmindful of their danger;

so the whangam ate the grasshopper; the serpent ate the whangam;

the yellow bird the serpent, and the hawk the yellow bird;

when, sousing from on high, a vulture gobbled up the hawk, grasshopper, whangam, and all in a moment."
